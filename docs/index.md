# Welcome to FujiNet

<div class="grid cards" markdown>

-   :material-wifi: **Connect to the internet**

    FujiNet gives your vintage computer instant Wi-Fi access — no soldering, no sneaker-net.

-   :material-floppy: **Browse thousands of programs**

    Mount disk images from curated online libraries the moment you power on.

-   :material-gamepad-variant: **Play with others**

    Cross-platform multiplayer games and global high-score tables, right from your vintage hardware.

-   :material-printer: **Replace a pile of hardware**

    One device emulates disk drives, tape, modem, printer, clock, and more simultaneously.

</div>

## Pick your platform to get started

=== ":material-controller: Atari 8-bit"

    FujiNet was born on the Atari 8-bit line and has the richest software library.

    [Get started with Atari 8-bit →](getting-started/atari-8bit.md){ .md-button .md-button--primary }

=== ":fontawesome-brands-apple: Apple II"

    FujiNet connects via the SmartPort bus on Apple II and II+ / IIe / IIc / IIgs machines.

    [Get started with Apple II →](getting-started/apple-ii.md){ .md-button .md-button--primary }

=== ":material-television-classic: Coleco ADAM"

    FujiNet attaches to the AdamNet bus alongside your existing ADAM peripherals.

    [Get started with Coleco ADAM →](getting-started/coleco-adam.md){ .md-button .md-button--primary }

=== ":material-controller: Commodore 64"

    FujiNet connects to the Commodore IEC bus, appearing as a fast network-connected drive.

    [Get started with Commodore 64 →](getting-started/commodore-64.md){ .md-button .md-button--primary }

=== ":material-television-play: CoCo"

    FujiNet brings the Tandy Color Computer into the world of Wi-Fi networking.

    [Get started with CoCo →](getting-started/coco.md){ .md-button .md-button--primary }
